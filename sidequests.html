Books

Unwell Women is a book by Elinor Cleghorn. It's a conversation starter on women's health, women's history in the medical field, as well as personal recounts of problems faced with her own reproductive rights from teenage years to motherhood. This book highlights the importance of speaking up for oneself especially in times when women are silenced by medical professionals and need to understand exactly what is going on with them.

I am I am I am is a memoir by Maggie O' Farrell and her many encounters with death. Starting off as a child with chronic illness, to the many confrontations in adulthood. Her most prominent essay is "Baby and Bloodstream (2005)" where she writes about her second miscarriage. Many women that go through a miscarriage are hit with a shock, needing comfort and support that most of the time isn't given by healthcare providers. It is necessary in this essay how much support she received from family members that were able to stand up for her as well as receive the care she deserved as a greiving woman.
